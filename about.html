About

Pixel Puppets is a live performance format built around real-time digital puppetry, surreal comedy, and audience interaction.

How it works

At the centre is a live act of puppetry. Puppeteers drive characters in real time, and the screen becomes a space where live performance is translated and warped. The audience does not simply watch from the outside. Their reactions, choices, and presence become part of how the performance unfolds.

Tone

Pixel Puppets draws on the aesthetic of familiar children’s television, then twists it for adult audiences. The worlds look bright, familiar, and dependable. Underneath, they run on the logic of a failing workplace, a hostile service economy, and a quietly fraying adult life. The format blends storybook framing, live camera language, game logic, broadcast-style interruptions, and surreal comedy.

Currently in Production

The first story in development is a twenty-minute R&D piece exploring the format in practice. It follows a village postman and his robo cat on a strange, comic delivery into a cheerfully hostile city.

Supporters

Pixel Puppets was developed through the BFI Immersive Fiction Lab. The project is now in funded research and development, supported by Arts Council England and Immersive Arts UK’s Experiment strand.

Team

Pixel Puppets is created by Ben Carlin, Artistic Director, and Joe Duggan, Producer. Ben has worked across virtual production, XR, and live performance for over a decade. Joe works across digital production and live performance, and pioneered the UK’s first large-scale VR theatre production.
