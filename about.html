About

Pixel Puppets is a live performance format built around real-time digital puppetry and audience interaction.

It draws on the language of familiar children’s television, then twists it. The worlds look bright, familiar, and dependable. Underneath, they run on the logic of a failing workplace, a hostile service economy, and a quietly fraying adult life. The format moves between storybook framing, live camera language, game logic, and broadcast-style interruptions.

At the centre is a live act of puppetry. Puppeteers drive the characters in real time, and the screen becomes a space where live performance is translated, warped, and shared with the audience. The audience do not watch from the outside. They are inside the system.

The first story in development is Shamazon Matt: City Run, a twenty-minute R&D piece exploring the format in practice. A village postman and his robo cat are sent on an out-of-area delivery into a cheerfully hostile city.

Pixel Puppets was developed through the BFI Immersive Fiction Lab. The project is now in funded research and development, supported by Arts Council England and Immersive Arts UK’s Experiment strand.

Pixel Puppets is created by Ben Carlin, Artistic Director, and Joe Duggan, Producer. Ben has worked across virtual production, XR, and live performance for over a decade. Joe is Head of Digital at the National Youth Theatre and led the UK’s first large-scale VR theatre production.
